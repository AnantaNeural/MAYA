**AnantaNeural Labs | Core Architecture Blueprint v1.0**

## 🧠 Abstract
Most contemporary AI systems are isolated in unimodal or bimodal digital environments (Text/Vision). At **AnantaNeural**, we are engineering **MAYA (Multimodal Artificial Yielding Agent)**—an Artificial General Intelligence designed to interact with the physical world through a biologically inspired framework. 

MAYA bypasses traditional backpropagation architectures. Instead, it utilizes **Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs)** combined with Karl Friston’s **Active Inference (Free Energy Principle)** to process real-time physical stimuli across five distinct sensory domains.

## 🏗️ The 5-Pillar Sensory Architecture

MAYA perceives reality by translating diverse physical inputs into a universal language: **Electrical Spikes**.

### 1. Vision (Computer Vision -> Spatial Spikes)
* **Hardware Interface:** Optical Lenses / Depth Cameras.
* **Mechanism:** Photons are converted into visual spike trains.
* **Function:** Spatial mapping, object recognition, and navigating complex terrain (e.g., underground mining environments).

### 2. Audition (Acoustic Sensors -> Frequency Spikes)
* **Hardware Interface:** High-fidelity Microphones / Acoustic Arrays.
* **Mechanism:** Soundwaves and frequency amplitudes are translated into auditory spikes.
* **Function:** Natural Language Processing (NLP), acoustic anomaly detection (e.g., machinery breakdown, roof-fall warnings).

### 3. Somatosensation (Tactile / e-Flesh -> Shear Spikes)
* **Hardware Interface:** e-Flesh / Piezoresistive tactile sensors.
* **Mechanism:** Mechanical compression and shear forces generate variable resistance, outputting tactile spikes.
* **Function:** Grip calibration, material texture recognition (hardness of rocks/minerals), and precision physical manipulation.

### 4. Gustation (Digital Taste / e-Tongue -> Liquid Chemical Spikes)
* **Hardware Interface:** Chemiresistors / Liquid Chemical Analyzers (Micro-digesters).
* **Mechanism:** Liquid chemical compositions alter sensor resistance, generating specific "taste" spike signatures.
* **Function:** Chemical analysis of liquids, slurry composition in mining, and biochemical testing.

### 5. Olfaction (Digital Smell / e-Nose -> Gas/VOC Spikes)
* **Hardware Interface:** VOC (Volatile Organic Compound) Sensors / Gas Detectors.
* **Mechanism:** Airborne chemicals interact with sensor coatings, generating olfactory spike patterns.
* **Function:** Pre-emptive hazard detection (e.g., identifying lethal Methane or Carbon Monoxide pockets before visual or auditory confirmation).

## ⚙️ The Cognitive Engine: Active Inference Loop
Instead of passively absorbing data, MAYA operates on the **Free Energy Principle**:
1. **Prior Prediction:** MAYA predicts the sensory input it expects to receive based on internal generative models.
2. **Sensory Surprise:** As the 5 sensors feed real-time spike data, discrepancies between the prediction and reality generate "Surprise" (Prediction Error).
3. **Action/Update:** MAYA either updates its internal model to learn from the new environment OR takes physical action (via robotic manipulators) to change the environment and minimize the error.
